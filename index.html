Introduction

Frank Evans Heart was a computer engineer who was extremely influential in computer networking. He led the team at Bolt, Beranek, and Newman (BBN) that built the Interface Message Processor (IMP), a foundational component of the ARPANET, which later evolved into the modern internet. The IMP acted as a packet-switching node, allowing computers to communicate over a network efficiently. Heart's leadership in developing the first reliable packet-switching hardware helped lay the groundwork for networked communication

By developing routing algorithms, I showed how computers could determine the best path for data to travel. This was crucial for ensuring that information reached its destination quickly and reliably. Timeline

August 1968

Heart is assigned as a project manager from ARPA to build the first Interface Message Processor.

September 6, 1968

Heart's team finalizes the nearly 200-page, $100,000 IMP proposal.

September 1, 1969

The first IMP is installed at the University of California, Los Angeles.

October 1, 1969

The second IMP is installed at the Stanford Research Institute in Menlo Park, California.

1971

Heart's IMP team transitions to a lighter Honeywell 316 for the IMP.

April 8, 2014

Heart is inducted into the Internet Hall of Fame.
